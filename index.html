Option 1 - The Quick and Easy

It's all in the title! This option is by far the fastest, for those of you that are looking to get rid of a comic book collection at speed, and with ease. The process here is simple.

  • What we need:
  • Estimate issue count (Does not have to be exact, but as a rough guide - a comic book long-box holds around 230 comic books if bagged and boarded, or 250 if unprotected)
  • Age range of publication (E.G 1960s, 1970s, 1980s etc. Again this can be a guess based on how long you, or the previous owner have owned the comic books.)
  • Estimate grade

Incredibly easy

With blind buys the offer per issue can be marginaly lower than a disclosed collection.

How to locate the publication date of a comic book?

Anooyingly, once more, this doesn't have a one-shot answer, though thankfully there is a slight consistency in location. With mMarvel comic books, and most Indie, the Publication date can be found on the first page, just inside the front cover.

With DC comics, many have the publication date just below the issue number, on the front cover.

Where do I locate the issue number?

Unfortunately there's no quick and easy answer to this, though in general the issue number should be fairly self explanitory and very easy to find.

On older marvel comics, in general the issue number will be located below the Marvel logo at the top left - though this is not inclusive of all covers.

With newer DC, again, in general the issue number will be located below the logo at the top left.
